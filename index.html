GLOBAL PATHS HELPS STUDENTS IDENTIFY THEIR GOALS AND DEVELOP THE SKILLS
TO ACHIEVE THEM.

11 days, 5 UK cities, global friendships, real-life experience!

July 15 - July 26, 2014

The Global Paths Summer Programme (GPS) is an exciting and enriching 11-day programme for 15-18 year old students from across the world.

"What should I do after secondary school?" "What are my goals in life and how do I reach them?"
"What type of university is the best fit for me?"
"A degree alone isn't enough anymore. What real-life skills are needed to succeed?"

Global Paths helps students to answer these questions and to develop the essential skills necessary to succeed in any career.
There are three main components of the GPS programme.

The Global Life Plan

Taking a step back, reflecting on the big picture and articulating your life goals whilst living on 4 university campuses. Advice is provided by higher education experts and thriving professionals from different organisations who share their own inspiring career paths and tips to success.

Read more
The Global Enterprise Project

Identifying an issue in any field and developing a solution. Students are mentored by professionals to develop their solution and present it to a panel.
Problem-solving, teamwork, flexibility, communication, innovation and creativity are developed!

Read more
A Cultural Experience

At the heart of GPS! We tour famous sights of London including Big Ben, Tower of London and Buckingham Palace. We also spend a day in Oxford, an afternoon in Lacock (where Harry Potter was filmed), play croquet and lots more!

ABOUT US

Helen Auckland created the Global Paths Summer Programme (GPS) to help students identify their goals and develop the skills to achieve them.
Her aim is to share her knowledge with students to help them make a non-biased, logical decision about what to do post-high school.
